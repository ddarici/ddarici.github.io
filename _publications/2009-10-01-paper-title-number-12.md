The answer has direct consequences for how quickly students develop procedural visual expertise, yet empirical evidence has been largely absent. <br><br> In a randomised controlled trial of 73 novice students, those in the extended coaching condition outperformed the extended modelling group by 12% in interpreting dynamic sonographic imagery and completed ultrasound examinations 7% faster. <br><br> The findings suggest that when first introducing new procedural skills, direct sensorimotor experience with guided support is more beneficial than extended observation.  For the development of visual expertise in particular, practice under supervision may accelerate the internalization of task-relevant gaze behavior in ways that watching an expert cannot replicate alone.'
